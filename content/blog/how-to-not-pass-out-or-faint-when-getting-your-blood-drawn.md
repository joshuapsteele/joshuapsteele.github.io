One of my least-favorite things about myself is that I'm a "fainter." Specifically, I tend to pass-out when I have to get my blood drawn (or an IV placed). Getting a shot isn't nearly as bad, although I do always make sure to look away.

I absolutely hate the feeling of cold, sweaty dread that comes over me when getting my blood drawn. It feels like there's absolutely nothing I can do to fight it. Indeed, the harder I try to fight it, the worse it often gets!

However, I have found a technique that's helped me to NOT pass-out the last, say, 2 or 3 times I've had my blood drawn. Basically it boils down to trying to keep my blood pressure elevated (instead of trying to stay calm, take deep breaths, etc.). 

Here's what I do:

- Tell myself it's OK to feel stressed-out, sweaty, and nervous.
- Try to channel this nervous energy into staying "amped-up": fidgeting, squeezing my hands into fists, flexing random muscles, etc.
- Let the phlebotomist or whomever else know that I'm a "fainter." Sometimes they'll have the ability to let you lie down. If not, at least they know what to expect.
- They usually tell me to try to calm down, slow my breathing, but I let them know that my strategy is to keep my blood pressure elevated so that I do not faint.
- In the final minute or so before the blood draw, I start crossing my ankles and flexing my legs and stomach muscles. I silently count to 5, relax and cross my ankles the other way, and then flex again and count to 5.
- Once the needle is in, I also flex my opposite arm along with my legs and stomach.
- Sometimes I've passed-out even after the needle is out, so I continue to do this flexing and counting routine until I feel like I'm good and definitely not going to faint.
